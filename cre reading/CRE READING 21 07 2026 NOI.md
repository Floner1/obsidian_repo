# NOI (Net operating income)
- operating profitability of specific property
- used as a proxy for cash flow when calculating debt service coverage ratios/ doing comps for market value
- number of add backs and normalizations required to understand the company's potential return for an investor
- usually investors will want to understate income to reduce tax, but overstate noi to imply higher property valuation

**Normalizing expenses to calculate noi:**
- 4 main categories that must be understood
1. non controllable:
	- property taxes, utilities, insurance, snow removal, security, concierge, etc.
	- non controllable bc if no pay then breach of contract
	- cash expenses, never added back to nibt when calculating noi
2. controllable:
	- repairs, maintanence, management and interest
		- can defer these payments to overstate nibt, or half ass the repairs or maintenance and still count it to reduce tax
	- property owners can pay themselves as little or much on management fees
	- debt as funding is optional, may not appear on all income statements depending for owner preference of low or high debt capital structures
3. non-cash:
	- depreciation is big one, not added back to nibt
4. Hypothetical:
	- aka vacancy allowance
	- expressed as % of rental income, simulate hypothetical downtime when property have no rental income
	- function of geography and property type, smaller communities and higher risk property classes commanding higher hypothetical vacancy rate
